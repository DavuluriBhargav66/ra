About Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941) was an Indian poet, philosopher, and polymath who became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913. His works are cherished worldwide for their spiritual depth, lyrical beauty, and humanist values. Tagore's contributions to literature, music, and education have made him a central figure in global cultural history.

Achievements

  • Nobel Prize in Literature (1913) for his collection *Gitanjali* (Song Offerings)
  • Founder of Visva-Bharati University in Shantiniketan, promoting holistic education
  • Composed the national anthems of India and Bangladesh

Legacy & Contributions

Rabindranath Tagore's work has left an indelible mark on literature and culture worldwide. His holistic approach to education through Shantiniketan, his advocacy for individual creativity, and his profound spiritual reflections continue to inspire generations across the globe.
